Basic Level:

2. How many unique products are in the dataset?
3. What are the top 5 most common product categories?
4. What is the distribution of orders across different cities?
5. How many unique customers are there?
6. What is the most frequent product category?
7. What is the average number of items per order?
8. What is the distribution of order statuses?
9. Which city has the highest number of orders?
10. What is the total number of orders delivered on time?

Intermediate Level:
1. What is the average order value (AOV) by product category?
2. Which customers have placed the highest number of orders?
3. What is the average delivery time for orders by city?
4. What is the most frequent payment method used by customers?
5. What is the total revenue for each seller?
6. How many products have no reviews?
7. What is the average review score for products?
8. Which seller has the highest sales volume (in terms of revenue)?
9. What is the distribution of reviews across different review scores?
10. What is the average price of products in each city?

Advanced Level:
1. What is the correlation between the order value and the shipping cost?
2. What is the impact of product reviews on sales?
3. What are the trends in the order status over time (e.g., delayed, canceled, completed)?
4. How can we segment customers based on their purchase behavior (e.g., frequency, monetary value)?
5. Can we detect seasonality in the sales data?
6. What factors contribute most to order delays?
7. What is the customer lifetime value (CLV) of the top 10% of customers?
8. What is the churn rate of customers in the past year?
9. What is the average shipping time by product category?
10. Can we predict the likelihood of an order being canceled based on its attributes?
